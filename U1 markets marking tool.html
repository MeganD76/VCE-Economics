5-Step Structure — Returning to Equilibrium

Original equilibrium — State starting P and Q. Use actual data values if provided; do not write P1/Q1 when numbers are given.

Factor and shift — Name the factor. D or S? Positive or negative? Direction of shift, name the curves.

Problem at original price — Quantities demanded vs supplied. Shortage or surplus?

Pressure and mechanism — Shortage → upward pressure (consumers bid up) | Surplus → downward pressure (producers discount). State contraction in demand / expansion in supply OR expansion in demand / contraction in supply.

New equilibrium — New P and Q. ↑D→↑P↑Q | ↓D→↓P↓Q | ↑S→↓P↑Q | ↓S→↑P↓Q

RP
Relative Prices — The Non-Negotiable Chain

Define — Relative prices = price of one good in terms of another. Resources allocated via demand and supply — the market mechanism.

Factor → price change — What changes D or S and what does this do to the relative price?

Price signal — Higher relative price = greater profit opportunity; lower = reduced profitability.

Profit motive → reallocation — Resources (land, labour, capital) directed towards higher profit markets and away from lower. For technical efficiency: relative input prices determine least-cost production method.

Link to concept — Allocative efficiency, technical efficiency, living standards, or the relevant basic economic question.

!
Common Errors
  • ❌ Using P1/Q1 when actual data is provided
  • ❌ Skipping contraction in demand / expansion in supply — just saying "a new equilibrium is reached"
  • ❌ Only one movement stated — both contraction AND expansion must be present
  • ❌ Relative prices: listing key terms without linking them causally
  • ❌ Relative prices: stopping at "price rises" without explaining the profit signal
  • ❌ Relative prices: assuming a higher absolute price always means a higher relative price — must compare across markets
  • ❌ Not linking the conclusion back to the concept in the question
